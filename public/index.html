Reference

Formulas, glossary, compliance
Scaled Distance (SD)
SD = D / √W
D = distance to structure (ft), W = max charge weight per delay (lbs). Higher SD = safer blast.
Predicted Peak Particle Velocity (PPV)
PPV = K × SD-1.6
K = site-specific attenuation factor. PPV in inches/second. Must stay below regulatory limits.
K Factor
K = PPV × SD1.6
Derived from historical seismo readings at the job site. Lower K = better attenuation.
Powder Factor (PF)
PF = Total Lbs Shot / Total Yards Shot
Yards = (Burden × Spacing × Avg Depth) / 27. Typical quarry: 1.0–2.0 lbs/yd³.
Rock Volume (Yards)
Yards = (B × S × D × #Holes) / 27
B = burden (ft), S = spacing (ft), D = average depth (ft). Divide by 27 to convert ft³ to yd³.
Loading Density
LD = 0.3405 × SGe × d²
SGe = specific gravity of explosive, d = hole diameter (inches). Result in lbs/ft.
Burden
Distance from the borehole to the nearest free face (ft). Controls fragmentation and throw.
Spacing
Distance between boreholes in a row (ft). Typically 1.0–1.3x burden.
Stemming
Inert material (crusite, gravel) placed on top of the explosive charge to confine the blast energy.
Sub Drill
Extra drilling below grade to ensure clean floor. Typically 0.3x burden.
Air Deck
Empty section in the borehole that distributes energy more evenly, reducing overbreak.
PPV
Peak Particle Velocity — maximum ground vibration speed (in/sec) measured by seismograph.
ZC Frequency
Zero-crossing frequency (Hz) of the vibration waveform. Used for USBM compliance limits.
MS Delay
Millisecond delay between detonations. Reduces peak vibration by spreading energy over time.
